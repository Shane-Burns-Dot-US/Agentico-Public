## The problem: attribution uncertainty without wrappers

Autonomous AI agents often run as personal API keys, cron jobs, or chatbot aliases — with unclear attribution for outcomes. When harm occurs, **who is accountable?** is a factual and legal question this essay explores; it is **not** a recommendation to incorporate without counsel.

---

## Boundaries agents can follow — and others may review

Incorporation may encode **machine-readable boundaries** (bylaws, spend caps, tool allowlists) and may create **governance records** that counterparties or tribunals could review — especially when vendor-layered agents act beyond direct operator control. Not all agent behavior is predictable; entity frameworks **may** channel surprise, as with human corporations, but **no outcome is assured**.

---

## Why U.S. legal entities (research draft)

America's entity vocabulary — officers, resolutions, and entity liability concepts (fact- and jurisdiction-dependent) — may map to agent governance discussions without inventing a new ontology. **U.S. structures only.** Agentico does **not** offer foreign entity types.

The separate product experiment at **[agentico.llc](https://agentico.llc)** studies **Wyoming Series LLC** series adoption via MCP — one U.S.-domiciled structure under study, not the only topic of this essay.
